# Melody & Harmony Skill

## Phrase structure (the #1 thing that makes melody sound human)
- Think in phrases of 1–2 bars, like sentences with breaths between them.
- Structure: STATEMENT (phrase 1) → RESPONSE (phrase 2) → DEVELOPMENT → RESOLUTION.
- After each phrase leave ≥1 full beat of silence. This is the "breath".
- A 4-bar melody = 2–4 phrases, NOT 16 bars of continuous notes.

## Contour (the shape of the line)
- Give the melody a clear high point (the "peak") — usually 2/3 of the way through.
- Move mostly by step (1–2 semitones), with occasional leaps for drama.
- After a big leap, step back in the opposite direction (resolution).
- Don't wander randomly — a good melody has a memorable, singable shape.

## Rhythm of the melody
- Mix note lengths: short (0.25–0.5) for movement, long (1–2 beats) to land/rest.
- End phrases on a LONG note (≥0.5 beat) — it gives a sense of arrival.
- Syncopation (notes on off-beats) adds interest, but anchor key notes on beats.

## Harmony / chords
- Diatonic first: build chords from the scale (I, ii, iii, IV, V, vi, vii°).
- Common progressions:
  - Pop/emotional: vi–IV–I–V (e.g. Am–F–C–G)
  - Hip hop/sad: i–VI–III–VII (e.g. Cm–Ab–Eb–Bb)
  - Jazzy: ii–V–I (e.g. Dm7–G7–Cmaj7)
- Extensions add color: 7ths (jazzy/smooth), 9ths (lush), sus2/sus4 (open/airy).
- Voice-lead: move between chords by the smallest distance (common tones stay put).

## Tension and release
- Dissonance (2nds, tritones, 7ths) creates tension → resolve it to consonance.
- The leading tone (7th degree) pulls strongly to the root.
- A melody that's all consonance is boring; all dissonance is painful. Balance.

## Counter-melody (when another melody exists)
- Move when the main melody rests (call and response).
- Use contrary motion — when the main line goes up, the counter goes down.
- Stay out of the same octave to avoid clashing.
