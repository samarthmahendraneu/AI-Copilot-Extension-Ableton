# House / Techno / Four-on-the-Floor Skill

## Tempo
- House: 120–128 BPM. Tech house: 124–128. Techno: 128–140. Deep house: 118–124.

## The four-on-the-floor foundation
- Kick: EVERY beat — 0.0, 1.0, 2.0, 3.0. Solid, consistent, the heartbeat.
- Open hat: the "tss" on the OFF-beats — 0.5, 1.5, 2.5, 3.5. This is essential.
- Closed hat: 16th fills between, softer (velocity 55–75).
- Clap/snare: beat 1.0 and 3.0 (the backbeat), often with reverb tail.

## Groove and swing
- House has subtle swing (52–56%). Techno is usually straight (50%).
- The shuffle comes from velocity and the off-beat open hat, not big timing shifts.

## Bass
- Off-beat bass stabs are classic house: bass on the "and" (0.5, 1.5, 2.5, 3.5),
  sitting in the pocket between kicks.
- Rolling 16th basslines for tech house — same note, rhythmic, hypnotic.
- Keep it simple and repetitive — house is about the groove, not the notes.

## Chords / stabs
- Off-beat chord stabs (the "house piano"). Short, punchy, on the up-beats.
- Major 7th and minor 7th chords. Bright, soulful for house; darker for techno.
- Pads: long, evolving, sit underneath everything.

## Arrangement
- Built on 8 and 16 bar phrases. Tension via filtering and adding/removing elements.
- Intro: drums only. Build by layering. Breakdown: drop the kick, let pads breathe.

## Velocity
- More consistent than hip hop (it's machine music), but still NOT flat.
- Kick: rock solid 110–120. Hats: alternate for the shuffle. Stabs: punchy 90–110.
