# Reggaeton / Dembow Skill

## Tempo
- Classic reggaeton: 80–95 BPM.
- Modern perreo / urbano latino: 90–100 BPM.
- Trap-reggaeton / RKT fusion: 88–96 BPM.

---

## The Dembow — the ONLY pattern that matters

The dembow is a syncopated kick pattern built on 8th-note positions.
In a 4-beat bar (0-indexed, 0.0–3.99):

**8th-note grid**: 0.0 | 0.5 | 1.0 | 1.5 | 2.0 | **2.5** | 3.0 | 3.5

The kick on **2.5** is THE dembow hit. It is non-negotiable. This is what makes it reggaeton.

### Classic Dembow (the base bar)
| Instrument | Beat positions | Velocity |
|---|---|---|
| Kick (bombo) | **0.0**, **2.5** | 115, 100 |
| Snare / Rim | 1.0, 3.0 | 90–100 |
| Closed hi-hat | 0.0, 0.5, 1.0, 1.5, 2.0, 2.5, 3.0, 3.5 (all 8ths) | 65–80 |
| Open hat | 3.5 | 95 |

### Perreo / Modern (denser kick)
| Instrument | Beat positions | Velocity |
|---|---|---|
| Kick | **0.0**, 1.75, **2.5** | 115, 85, 100 |
| Snare / Rim | 1.0, 3.0 | 90–100 |
| Closed hi-hat | all 16ths (0.0, 0.25, 0.5 … 3.75) | alternating 75 / 55 |
| Open hat | 3.5 | 95 |

The 1.75 kick adds a "push" before the dembow hit — it's a signature of modern perreo.

### Trap-Reggaeton
| Instrument | Beat positions | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Kick | 0.0, 0.75, **2.5** | Extra 0.75 adds syncopation |
| Snare / Clap | 1.0, 3.0 | Hard backbeat |
| 808 bass | locked to 0.0 and 2.5 | Long, gliding sub — same as trap |
| Hats | 16th rolls: 1.0→1.125→1.25, 3.0→3.125→3.25 | Rising velocity trap-style |

---

## Swing
**NONE. Zero. Straight 16ths (50%).** Do NOT add swing.
The groove in reggaeton comes entirely from syncopation — the dembow kick placement.
Swing destroys the feel. This is the opposite of hip hop.

---

## Velocity
- Kick at 0.0: **110–120** — loudest element, the anchor
- Kick at 2.5 (dembow): **95–108** — prominent but slightly softer than the downbeat
- Snare/rim: 85–100 — solid, a little mechanical (reggaeton is precise)
- Closed hat: 65–80 — consistent, machine-like (no humanization on hats)
- Open hat: 90–105 — the "lift" before the bar repeats

---

## The dembow self-check
Before submitting any pattern, verify:
1. ✅ Kick fires at beat 2.5 — if not, it is NOT reggaeton
2. ✅ Straight 16ths — no swing offset
3. ✅ Snare on 1.0 and 3.0
4. ✅ Hats are consistent and mechanical (not humanized or swung)

Moving the dembow kick to 2.0 → sounds like pop. Removing it → sounds like basic 4/4.

---

## Bass
- Root note locked to kick hits: bass hits at 0.0 and 2.5.
- One-note, hypnotic bass is authentic — reggaeton bass rarely changes notes mid-bar.
- Long sustained sub at 2.5, short punchy hit at 0.0.
- Modern reggaeton: 808-style sub bass (same rules as trap 808).

---

## Fills and variation_bars
- Use variation_bars only on bar 3 of a 4-bar phrase.
- Fill = rimshot/snare roll in beats 3.25, 3.5, 3.75 (the last 3 16ths of the bar).
- Crash hit on beat 0.0 of bar 0 to mark the phrase restart.
- Fills must be subtle — the dembow IS the texture. Over-filling = wrong genre feel.
