# Reggaeton / Dembow Reference

Genre conventions — adapt to the user's prompt; these are starting points, not rules.

## Tempo
- Classic reggaeton: 80–95 BPM.
- Modern perreo / urbano latino: 90–100 BPM.
- Trap-reggaeton / RKT fusion: 88–96 BPM.

---

## The Dembow — the defining rhythmic pattern

The dembow is a syncopated kick pattern built on 8th-note positions.
In a 4-beat bar (0-indexed, 0.0–3.99):

**8th-note grid**: 0.0 | 0.5 | 1.0 | 1.5 | 2.0 | 2.5 | 3.0 | 3.5

A kick around **beat 2.5** (the 6th 8th note) is the characteristic dembow hit — the element
most associated with reggaeton's feel. Removing or moving it tends to shift the genre toward
generic pop or dancehall. Keep it unless the prompt specifically asks for something different.

### Classic Dembow (the base bar)
| Instrument | Beat positions | Velocity |
|---|---|---|
| Kick (bombo) | 0.0, 2.5 | 115, 100 |
| Snare / Rim | 1.0, 3.0 | 90–100 |
| Closed hi-hat | 0.0, 0.5, 1.0, 1.5, 2.0, 2.5, 3.0, 3.5 (all 8ths) | 65–80 |
| Open hat | 3.5 | 95 |

### Perreo / Modern (denser kick)
| Instrument | Beat positions | Velocity |
|---|---|---|
| Kick | 0.0, 1.75, 2.5 | 115, 85, 100 |
| Snare / Rim | 1.0, 3.0 | 90–100 |
| Closed hi-hat | all 16ths (0.0, 0.25, 0.5 … 3.75) | alternating 75 / 55 |
| Open hat | 3.5 | 95 |

The 1.75 kick adds a "push" before the dembow hit — a signature of modern perreo.

### Trap-Reggaeton
| Instrument | Beat positions | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Kick | 0.0, 0.75, 2.5 | Extra 0.75 adds syncopation |
| Snare / Clap | 1.0, 3.0 | Hard backbeat |
| 808 bass | aligned to 0.0 and 2.5 | Long, gliding sub — same as trap |
| Hats | 16th rolls: 1.0→1.125→1.25, 3.0→3.125→3.25 | Rising velocity trap-style |

---

## Swing
Reggaeton is conventionally straight 16ths (no swing). The groove comes from the syncopated
kick placement rather than timing offsets — which is the opposite of hip hop. That said,
slight swing can work in slower, more laid-back variants if the prompt calls for it.

---

## Velocity
- Kick at 0.0: 110–120 — the main anchor
- Kick at 2.5 (dembow): 95–108 — prominent but slightly softer than the downbeat
- Snare/rim: 85–100 — solid, fairly mechanical (reggaeton is precise)
- Closed hat: 65–80 — consistent, machine-like
- Open hat: 90–105 — the lift before the bar repeats

---

## Bass
- Bass root typically aligns to kick hits at 0.0 and 2.5.
- One-note, hypnotic bass is authentic — reggaeton bass rarely changes notes mid-bar.
- Long sustained sub at 2.5, short punchy hit at 0.0.
- Modern reggaeton often uses 808-style sub bass.

---

## Fills and variation_bars
- Fills typically land on bar 3 of a 4-bar phrase.
- Fill = rimshot/snare roll in beats 3.25, 3.5, 3.75 (the last 3 16ths of the bar).
- Crash hit on beat 0.0 of bar 0 to mark the phrase restart.
- Fills tend to be subtle — the dembow pattern itself carries the texture.
