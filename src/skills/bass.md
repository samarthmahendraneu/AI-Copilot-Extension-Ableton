# Bassline Skill

## The bass has two jobs
1. **Rhythmic**: lock with the kick drum. They are a rhythm-section unit.
2. **Harmonic**: state the root of each chord, defining the harmony's foundation.

## Locking to the kick (critical)
- The first bass note of each chord lands ON the kick (usually beat 0.0).
- Where the kick hits, the bass usually hits too (or sustains through).
- Look at the drum pattern FIRST, then place bass notes to align with kicks.

## Note choice
- Root note on the downbeat — always. This grounds the harmony.
- Then: 5th (strong), octave (movement), or passing tones (3rd, b7) for walking lines.
- Avoid the 3rd on strong beats unless intentional — it muddies with chords above.

## Register
- Keep it LOW: roughly MIDI 28–48 (E0–C2). One bass note per moment — no chords.
- If it's too high it competes with the melody; too low it disappears. Sweet spot ~C1.

## Genre rhythms
- Hip hop/trap 808: long held roots, 1–2 per bar, sliding between them.
- House: off-beat stabs (the "and" of each beat) OR rolling 16th notes.
- Funk: syncopated, ghost notes, lots of 16th-note movement and space.
- Reggae/dub: sparse, deep, locked to the "one drop".

## Movement
- Static (one root held) = solid, modern, hip hop.
- Walking (stepwise motion connecting chords) = jazz, soul, funk.
- A good bassline implies the chord changes even with no chords present.

## Velocity
- Fairly even (it's a foundation), 85–110, but accent the downbeat root slightly.
- Ghost notes (soft 40–60) between main notes add funk groove.
