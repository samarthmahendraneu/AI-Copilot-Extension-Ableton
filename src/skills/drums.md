# Drum Programming Skill

## The bar-repeating contract
- You define ONE base bar. The engine clones it across every bar.
- The base bar MUST contain the full kick + snare + hat backbone.
- variation_bars ADD hits on top (fills, crashes) — they never remove the backbone.
- Therefore: put EVERY essential hit in base_pattern. Never rely on variations for groove.

## The backbone (build in this order)
1. **Kick** — anchor on beat 0 (downbeat). Add 1–3 more per bar depending on genre.
2. **Snare/Clap** — the backbeat. Almost always beats 2.0 and 4.0... wait, in 4/4
   with beats numbered 0-3: snare on beat 1.0 and 3.0 (the "2 and 4" of the bar).
3. **Hats** — closed hats fill the subdivision (8ths or 16ths). This sets the energy.
4. **Accents** — open hat, ride, ghost snares, shaker for human feel.

## Snare placement (this is where most mistakes happen)
- In a 4-beat bar (0,1,2,3): the backbeat snare sits on beat **1.0 and 3.0**.
- These two snares are SACRED. They must be in base_pattern and never move.
- Fills (extra snare hits) go in variation_bars and STACK on top — the beat 1.0
  and 3.0 snares still play underneath.

## Velocity for drums
- Kick: 100–120 (consistent, it's the anchor)
- Snare backbone: 95–115. Ghost snares: 25–50.
- Closed hat: alternate 70–90 / 50–70 to create a "tick-TOCK" groove.
- Open hat / ride: 85–105 (they ring out, they're features).

## Fills
- Only on the LAST bar of a 4 or 8 bar phrase (bar index 3 or 7).
- A fill ADDS snare/tom hits in the 2nd half of the bar (beats 3.0–3.75).
- The base kick + the beat-1.0 and beat-3.0 snares STILL PLAY during a fill.

## Common mistakes to avoid
- ❌ Putting the backbone snare in variation_bars (it will only play that one bar)
- ❌ Moving the snare off the backbeat to "be creative" (it kills the groove)
- ❌ Same velocity on every hat (sounds robotic and cheap)
- ❌ Hats on every 16th with no velocity variation (fatiguing, lifeless)
